Other notable projects

Attenuating Gender Stereotype using LinkedIn research proposal
Jan - May '21


Abstract

Research on gender inequalities within leadership roles has been popularly explained by the ceiling effect which describes how gender stereotypes prevent women from attaining positions of power. Using the Stereotype Content Model (SCM), this study will explore whether observing a female succeeding in a leadership position will be enough to mitigate these stereotypes through associations between status and competence. Incorporating modern-day recruiting tool LinkedIn in the experimental design, we will test how the gender of a successful CEO affects which candidates are chosen and how candidates are assessed on dimensions of the SCM and leadership qualities. This study will also explore whether bias from physical attractiveness plays a role when gender stereotypes have been mitigated. The value of this research will contribute to how gender stereotypes are reinforced and can be mitigated as well as question the value of photos in social media involved in modern-day recruiting.

Keywords: Recruitment, Gender Inequality, Stereotype Content Model, Physical Attraction
